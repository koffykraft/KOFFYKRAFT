Lesson 1: Introduction to Coffee and Brewing Basics

Learning Goal

Understand what coffee is, what brewing means, and why good coffee is achievable at home.

Core Concept

Coffee is the dried seed of a fruit. Roasting develops flavor. Brewing uses water to dissolve desirable flavors from ground coffee into the cup.

  • Black coffee: brewed coffee without milk, such as pour-over, drip coffee, French press, or espresso.
  • Milk-based coffee: brewed coffee or espresso combined with milk, such as latte or cappuccino.
  • Brewing mindset: you are not trying to be perfect. You are learning how water, coffee, grind, and time change flavor.
Try This
  1. Break or crush a roasted coffee bean and smell it.
  2. Smell the same coffee after grinding. Notice how the aroma becomes stronger.
  3. Put a pinch of ground coffee in room-temperature water and observe what happens.
  4. Put a pinch of ground coffee in hot water and compare how quickly the water changes color.

This simple exercise shows why grinding and hot water make extraction faster.

Self-check

1. What are the two main categories of coffee beverages?

Answer: Black coffee and milk-based coffee.

2. Why do we grind coffee beans before brewing?

Answer: Grinding increases surface area, helping water extract flavor more effectively.

3. True or false: beginners are allowed to make mistakes while learning to brew.

Answer: True. Mistakes are part of learning and help you understand what to adjust next time.

4. What is the role of water in brewing?

Answer: Water is the solvent. It pulls soluble flavor compounds from the coffee grounds into the drink.

Reflection

What excites you most about learning to brew coffee? Write one short reason. Keep it and look back after a few lessons.

Beginner caution

Recipes and numbers are starting points. Grind, water, equipment, roast level, dose, agitation, and taste decide the final adjustment.

Before you continue

  • Write the main idea in one sentence.
  • Try one small exercise or observation from the lesson.
  • Record what you tasted, smelled, changed, or noticed without judging yourself.
