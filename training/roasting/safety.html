Safety and Setup for Self-Study Roasting

Roasting uses high heat, moving parts, smoke, chaff, and rapidly changing coffee chemistry. Learn the process with care before chasing profile numbers.

Non-negotiables

  • Never leave a roaster unattended while it is heating, roasting, or cooling.
  • Roast only with strong ventilation. Smoke and roasting gases must be moved away from people and enclosed spaces.
  • Control chaff. Dry chaff can ignite if it collects near heat or airflow paths.
  • Know your cooling procedure before starting the batch.
  • Keep fire-safety equipment nearby and follow your machine manual.
  • Do not touch hot metal, probes, trays, or exhaust parts without protection.

Machine variation

Displayed temperatures are not universal. Drum temperature, environmental temperature, inlet temperature, bean probe temperature, and actual internal bean temperature are different measurements. Probe placement, batch size, airflow, charge routine, and software smoothing can shift the numbers you see.

Use sensory order first

Learn the sequence: green smell, yellowing, bread-like aroma, browning, First Crack, development aromas, drop, cooling, rest, and cup result. Then calibrate your machine numbers to that sequence.

Before your first practical roast

  • Read the machine manual.
  • Run an empty warm-up and cooling check if your equipment allows it.
  • Prepare a log sheet with time, temperature, gas/heat, airflow, color, aroma, sound, and notes.
  • Set a conservative plan and change only one major variable in the next roast.
