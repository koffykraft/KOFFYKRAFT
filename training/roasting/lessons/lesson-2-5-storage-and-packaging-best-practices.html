Applied Control / Lesson 2.5

Storage & Packaging Best Practices

The roasting learning loop

  1. Intend: name the coffee, brew use, and flavor goal before heating the machine.
  2. Plan: choose batch size, charge approach, airflow, milestones, and one variable to watch.
  3. Roast and log: record time, temperature, colour, aroma, sound, airflow, gas or power, and decisions.
  4. Cup: evaluate with the Cupping course discipline before defending the profile.
  5. Brew: test with a realistic brew method from the Brewing course.
  6. Revise: change one thing in the next roast and write why.
What this lesson teaches

Coffee keeps changing after roasting. Degassing, aroma stabilization, packaging, and brew method affect when a coffee tastes best.

How this builds the student

This lesson builds post-roast patience.

Cupping connection

Use aroma, sweetness, acidity, body, aftertaste, balance, defects, and confidence notes from the Cupping course. A roast decision is not proven until the cup supports it.

Brewing connection

Check whether the roast extracts sensibly in a real recipe. Sour, hollow, bitter, harsh, or muddy brews may point back to roast development, solubility, or brew setup.

From the KoffyKraft notes

Learning Goal

Understand how to store roasted coffee and select packaging that maintains freshness, preserves flavor, and respects origin quality.

A. Why Storage & Packaging Matter

  • Poor storage = lost aroma, staling, and dull cups- Proper storage maintains sweetness, body, and aroma- Essential for roasters, cafes, and home brewers alike

B. Key Enemies of Freshness

| Enemy | Effect on Coffee | Solution ||-----------|-----------------------------------|----------------------------------------|| Oxygen | Oxidation, staling | Use valve bags or nitrogen flush || Moisture | Mold, sour flavors | Dry environment, sealed packs || Heat | Accelerates aging | Store cool (18-22 deg C), avoid sunlight || Light | Destroys aromatics | Use opaque packaging || Time | Natural degradation | Sell/consume within 2-4 weeks |

C. Optimal Storage Conditions

  • See infographic: Flavor Retention Curve under Different Conditions (0-30 Days)| Factor | Ideal Value ||--------------|------------------------------------|| Temperature | 18-22 deg C || Humidity | 50-60% RH || Container | Airtight, food-safe, inert || Light | Opaque or dark storage || Airflow | Sealed or low-oxygen environment |

D. Packaging Types and Trade-offs

| Package Type | Use Case | Notes ||------------------------|---------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|| Kraft Paper Bags | Short-term, cafes | Cheap, not airtight, good for 1-2 days || Foil-Lined Valve Bags | Retail, home use | Standard; blocks oxygen/light, good for 2-4 weeks || Compostable Pouches | Eco-conscious brands | Less barrier; use quickly, store cool/dry || Resealable Bags | Daily brewing | Useful for cafes or home baristas || Nitrogen-Flushed Packs | Export, long shelf life | Longest shelf life; higher cost, lab packaging setup |

E. Degassing and Sealing Timing by Origin

| Roast Type | Bean Density / Origin Example | Seal Time (Days) | Note ||--------------------|-------------------------------|---------------------------|------------------------------------|| Light (Filter) | High-density - Ethiopia | Day 2-3 | Slow degassing, floral retention || Medium | Mid-density - Colombia | Day 2 | Balanced aroma & body || Dark (Espresso) | Low-density - Robusta | Day 1 | Fast degassing, rich crema |

F. Storage Strategy by Setup + Troubleshooting

| Setup | Strategy | Troubleshooting Tip ||-----------------|----------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|| Home Roaster | Airtight jars, label roast dates, use quickly | Bag bloats? -> Wait longer before sealing || Small Cafe | Daily-use containers, bulk in foil pouches | Coffee tastes dull? -> Check temp & humidity exposure || Retail Roastery | Foil valve bags, batch roast, rotate stock often | Bitter notes? -> Check exposure to light/heat || Export Roaster | Nitrogen flush + vacuum seal, 3-month rotation | Early fade? -> Recheck packaging seals |

G. Beginner Analogy: Coffee Is Like Bread

  • Fresh = aromatic and flavorful- Exposed = dries out, becomes flat- Store in sealed packaging, just like bread or baked goods

H. Real-World Practice (Tasting Grid Example)

  • Source 100g each of light and medium roasts (e.g., Blue Tokai)- Store one sealed in drawer, other opened daily and exposed to sunlight- Brew 10g via V60 at Day 7 and Day 14, compare| Day | Storage Style | Aroma | Acidity | Sweetness | Body | Notes ||-----|----------------------|---------|---------|------------|------|--------------------|| 7 | Sealed, cool drawer | Floral | Bright | Balanced | Clean| Still vibrant || 7 | Open + sunlight | Dull | Low | Weak | Thin | Slightly stale || 14 | Sealed, cool drawer | Mild | Balanced| Medium | Smooth| Still enjoyable || 14 | Open + sunlight | Faint | Flat | None | Flat | Stale, not usable |

J. Sustainability Note

  • Better packaging = longer shelf = less waste- Preserved origin flavor = fair pricing for smallholders- Compostable and recyclable packaging help reduce landfill waste

K. Sample Reflection Prompt

Prompt 1: 'My coffee turned flat in a week.'Answer: Possibly stored near heat or light. Try opaque, valve-sealed bag in a cool, dry place.Prompt 2: 'Bags were bloated the next day.'Answer: Too early sealing. Wait 24-72 hours depending on roast and origin.

L. Glossary

  • Degassing: CO2 escape that occurs post-roast- Oxidation: Exposure to air that flattens flavor- Valve Bag: Bag with one-way vent for safe degassing- Compostable: Biodegradable packaging material- Staling: Loss of volatile flavor due to storage exposure

Practice assignment

  1. State the roast intention in one sentence.
  2. Roast, observe, or study a roast video with the lesson idea in mind.
  3. Cup the result or a comparable coffee using a simple written form.
  4. Brew the same coffee with one normal method and compare it to the cupping result.
  5. Write one next-roast change and one reason not to change anything else.

Common beginner mistakes

  • Trusting the curve before tasting the coffee.
  • Changing charge, airflow, batch size, and development all at once.
  • Using one roast defect word as a verdict without checking green coffee, machine, log, and cup.
  • Ignoring brew behavior after a roast cups well.

Self-check with answer guide

1. What should this lesson change in your roasting judgment?

Answer: It should give you one clearer observation or decision rule, not a memorized number.

2. How do cupping and brewing protect you from false confidence?

Answer: Cupping isolates roast and green coffee signals; brewing tests whether the roast works in the way someone will actually drink it.

3. What is the safest next experiment?

Answer: Repeat the roast with one planned change, keep the same evaluation method, and compare notes side by side.
