Quality and Improvement Notes

These are notes for future editing, trials, translation, and scientific strengthening.

Improvements already applied

  • Keep the LRN origin story as history, but separate it from proven soil-health principles. The public handbook now labels LRN as a local field framework built on established principles.
  • Avoid claims such as guaranteed yield increase, guaranteed disease suppression, or universal input replacement. The site frames outcomes as observable indicators and trial results.
  • Use 'supports', 'can help', and 'is associated with' for biology claims unless a cited source supports a stronger statement.
  • Add local crop trials later: date, soil type, rainfall, crop, treatment, comparison, yield, pest pressure, and photos.
  • Create printable Malayalam field cards later if the target audience includes Kerala field workers.
  • Add a formal glossary and diagrams later for apoplast, symplast, rhizosphere, mycorrhiza, EC, and nutrient antagonism.
  • Where LRN documents mention specific homemade inputs, publish only after recipe, safety, dose, storage, and crop-stage limits are checked.

Next evidence upgrades

Add local trial tables, rainfall records, soil test ranges, crop-stage photos, and farmer observations. That will move the handbook from a strong learning base to a regionally validated operating manual.
