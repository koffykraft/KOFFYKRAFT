Quality and Beginner-Friendliness Notes

These notes summarize the content scan before page generation. The lesson pages include caution boxes and checkpoints based on this review.

Main findings

  • Cupping should be framed as observation practice, not a test or performance.
  • Scores can intimidate beginners; descriptive notes should be allowed before numeric scoring.
  • Defect language should be diagnostic and cautious, not a verdict from one taste.
  • Real-life cupping conditions matter: lighting, mood, fatigue, food, fragrance, and room noise can change perception.
  • Shared cupping needs hygiene guidance for spoons, cups, spitting, allergies, caffeine load, and clean water.

Editing approach

Keep the original teaching context, but avoid rigid claims where coffee depends on equipment, water, roast level, palate, environment, or repeated tasting.
