KOFFY KRAFT – Cupping Manual for Beginners

This guide walks you through how to use the Koffy Kraft Cupping Form. It's designed for beginners, whether you're cupping at home or with friends, with or without fancy tools.

Session Details

Name, Date, Time: Helps track when and who did the cupping.- Coffee Name, Origin, Roast Level: Log the type of coffee you're tasting.- Brew Method: How you brewed it – French Press, V60, etc.- Brew Ratio: Common ratios are 1:15 or 1:16 (coffee to water by weight).- Water Temp & Brew Temp: Ideal range is 90–96°C. Note both if possible.- TDS: If you have a TDS meter, enter the reading. Skip if you don’t.- Grind Size: Note whether it’s fine, medium, coarse, etc.- Altitude: Higher-grown beans often taste brighter. Optional.- Notes: Anything unusual or worth remembering.

Cupping Evaluation

Use a score between 1 (poor) to 10 (excellent). Taste, smell, and observe carefully.

Aroma: Smell before and after brewing. Fragrant? Dull?- Acidity: Pleasant brightness (like citrus) or sourness?- Sweetness: Natural sweetness, like honey or fruit.- Body: The weight or thickness of the brew in your mouth.- Aftertaste: What lingers after you swallow.- Balance: Do the flavors work well together?- Uniformity: Do all sips taste the same?- Clean Cup: Free of off-flavors or defects?- Overall: Your general impression.- Defects: Anything unpleasant – grassy, burnt, rubbery, etc.

Tips for First-Time Cuppers

Don’t worry about being wrong – taste is personal.- Compare with others and talk about what you sense.- Try to cup different roasts or origins side by side.- Slurp (yes, really!) to spread the coffee across your tongue.- Use consistent water and brewing equipment when you can.

Beginner caution

Cupping is a noticing practice, not an exam. Scores and flavor words should support honest observation, not pressure to sound professional.

Before you continue

  • Write the main idea in one sentence.
  • Try one small exercise or observation from the lesson.
  • Record what you tasted, smelled, changed, or noticed without judging yourself.
